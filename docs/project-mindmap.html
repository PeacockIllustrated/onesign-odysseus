II.

The Voyage · a job from enquiry to installed

eleven ports · one homecoming

A typical job sails through eleven ports. Enquiry lands with Onesign by phone or email; Ithaca is the sign, lit and signed off at the client's building. Each port shows who does the work and what the next step is. Gold markers are client-facing moments; amber markers are where Onesign staff still lift a pen.

III.

Inside Production · where the making happens

thirteen stages · one job board

Production is the heart of the business — and the widest part of Odysseus. Every accepted job flows through a pipeline of thirteen stages, grouped into three phases: queue & approve, make, and ship. A single job only visits the craft stages it actually needs; a painted fabricated sign will tour Metal Fab → Painters → Lighting → Assembly, while a cut vinyl graphic might only pass through Vinyl. One job board shows them all at once.

Routing is per-item, not per-job

Each sub-item on an artwork job carries a target stage. When the artwork is released, the job's route through the workshop is built from those choices — a job only queues at the stations its items actually need.

Artwork approval gates production

Nothing enters the make phase until the client has signed off on the artwork. Stage 02 is the gate — before it, cards sit in the order book; after it, the routing is live on the shop floor.

Office and workshop, same data

The admin job board shows every stage at once. Each shop-floor station has its own tablet view showing only that station's queue. They're two views of the same live data, not two systems.

Goods Out triggers the next voyage

When the last item of a job reaches Goods Out, a delivery record is auto-created with the client's site address already on it. Production closes; the next chapter begins.

IV.

Inside Delivery · the last mile

four phases · one signature at the end

When a job reaches Goods Out, Odysseus creates the delivery record automatically — the site address and the item list are already on it. From there a driver and date are assigned, the van goes out, and the client signs on site. Four phases, one proof-of-delivery link, no clipboards.

Deliveries create themselves

When the last item of a job reaches Goods Out, Odysseus creates a delivery record with the client, site address, contact, and items already attached. The admin never starts from a blank form.

The driver and the client both sign

The proof-of-delivery page captures two signatures at the site: the driver's and the client's. They're stored against the delivery record as evidence that the sign has been handed over.

The link is there — the send isn't

The one-time link to the PoD page is generated, but today it has to be sent to the driver or site contact by hand. Wiring email closes the only remaining manual step in the voyage.

Every driver has a home postcode

Drivers are geocoded from their home postcode so route planning can factor in where each day begins and ends. Four drivers on file today.

V.

Notes on the chart

One platform, one flow

Odysseus replaces what used to be spread across spreadsheets, Clarity Go, and shoulder-tapping. A job enters as a quote and exits as a signed-off installation — without ever leaving the system.

The client's details follow the job

Every step knows which client it belongs to, which contact to call, and which site to deliver to. When a delivery goes out, the address travelled with it from the quote — no re-keying, no mistakes.

Artwork is where the spec lives

The artwork module isn't just files — it's the spec sheet. Material, dimensions, finish, and which department will make it are all captured there, so once a client signs off, the shop floor already knows what's coming.

Two gaps to close

Today the approval link and the proof-of-delivery link both get sent by hand. Wiring email onto those two moments removes the most friction in the whole voyage — one small job, big daily payoff.

Departments on the same screen

All thirteen departments share one job board. A job waiting at Paint is visible to everyone; when it moves to Assembly, it shows up there automatically. Nothing hides.

The client never logs in

Clients get secure one-time links for artwork approval and delivery sign-off — no accounts, no passwords. Odysseus is a tool for Onesign staff; the client experience is invisible and friction-free.

Quote or artwork — either can come first

Some jobs start with a price conversation; others start with a design someone already has in their head. The plan is to make Quoting and Artwork interchangeable as starting points — a button on each to spin up the other. Whichever order the work arrives in, Odysseus meets it there.
